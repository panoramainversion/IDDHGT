# A Dynamic Partition Theory of the Riemann Zeta Function via Finite Euler Products

## Abstract
This article proposes a dynamic partition framework for the Riemann Zeta function $\zeta(s)$. By introducing a truncation parameter $N$, we partition the set of all positive integers into three mutually exclusive arithmetic subsets, thereby decomposing $\zeta(s)$ into three regional functions: the Core term $R_1$, the Smooth term $R_2$, and the Global Residue term $R_3$. Furthermore, we define a Differential Evolution Operator $\Phi$ to describe the system's evolutionary behavior as the truncation scale $N$ increases. This framework reveals the dynamical mechanism of non-trivial zeros migrating from the imaginary axis $\mathrm{Re}(s)=0$ to the critical line $\mathrm{Re}(s)=\frac{1}{2}$.

---

## 1. Preliminaries and Notations

Let $s = \sigma + it \in \mathbb{C}$. Let $\mathbb{P}$ denote the set of all prime numbers. For any positive integer truncation $N \in \mathbb{Z}^+$, we define the following arithmetic quantities:

* **Truncated Prime Set:** $\mathbb{P}_N = \{ p \in \mathbb{P} \mid p \leq N \}$
* **$p$-adic Valuation Index:** $a_p = v_p(N) = \max \{ k \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0} : p^k \mid N \}$
* **$N$-Smooth Number Set:** $S_N = \{ n \in \mathbb{Z}^+ \mid p \mid n \implies p \leq N \}$
    * This set contains all positive integers whose prime factors do not exceed $N$.

---

## 2. Analytical Decomposition of Regional Functions

Based on the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, we partition the Dirichlet series expansion of $\zeta(s)$ (valid for $\mathrm{Re}(s) > 1$) into three sub-series with distinct arithmetic significance.

### 2.1 The Core Region $R_1$ (Divisor Generating Function)
$R_1(s; N)$ is defined as the finite series over all positive divisors of $N$:
$$R_1(s; N) = \sum_{d \mid N} \frac{1}{d^s} = \prod_{p \in \mathbb{P}_N} \frac{1 - p^{-s(a_p+1)}}{1 - p^{-s}}$$
This region represents the fully determined "known" part of the system at the current "grammatical depth" $N$.

### 2.2 The Transition Region $R_2$ (Smooth Non-Divisor Terms)
$R_2(s; N)$ is defined as the series over all $N$-smooth numbers that do not divide $N$:
$$R_2(s; N) = \sum_{\substack{n \in S_N \\ n \nmid N}} \frac{1}{n^s} = \prod_{p \in \mathbb{P}_N} \frac{1}{1 - p^{-s}} - \prod_{p \in \mathbb{P}_N} \frac{1 - p^{-s(a_p+1)}}{1 - p^{-s}}$$
This region represents potential terms generated by known primes that have not entered the Core due to exceeding the valuation threshold.

### 2.3 The Global Residue Region $R_3$ (High-Dimensional Prime Terms)
$R_3(s; N)$ is defined as the series over all numbers containing at least one prime factor strictly greater than $N$:
$$R_3(s; N) = \sum_{n \notin S_N} \frac{1}{n^s} = \zeta(s) - \prod_{p \in \mathbb{P}_N} \frac{1}{1 - p^{-s}}$$

### 2.4 Global Decomposition Identity
**Theorem 2.1.** For any $N \geq 1$ and $\mathrm{Re}(s) > 1$, there exists a unique decomposition of $\zeta(s)$:
$$\zeta(s) = R_1(s; N) + R_2(s; N) + R_3(s; N)$$

---

## 3. The Differential Evolution Operator $\Phi$

We define the expansion operator $\Phi$ that governs the transition of the system from truncation scale $N$ to $N'$:
$$\Phi_{N \to N'}(s) = \frac{R_1(s; N')}{R_1(s; N)}$$

The evolution is realized through two paths:
* **Dimensional Activation (Path A):** Introduction of a new prime $q > N$.
* **Depth Evolution (Path B):** Incrementing the valuation $a_p$ of an existing prime $p \leq N$.

The growth of the system is driven by the multiplicative equation: $R_1(s; N') = R_1(s; N) \cdot \Phi_{N \to N'}(s)$.

---

## 4. Asymptotic Limits and Convergence

**Theorem 4.1.** Under the condition $N \to \infty$ and $\min(a_p) \to \infty$:
1.  $\lim_{N \to \infty} R_2(s; N) = 0$
2.  $\lim_{N \to \infty} R_3(s; N) = 0$
3.  $\lim_{N \to \infty} R_1(s; N) = \zeta(s)$

This demonstrates that the finite arithmetic structure becomes exactly isomorphic to the infinite Zeta function in the limit.

---

## 5. Zero-Migration Theory

### 5.1 Initial Positions of Zeros
**Proposition 5.1.** All roots of $R_1(s; N)$ (the "original zeros") are strictly distributed on the imaginary axis $\mathrm{Re}(s) = 0$.

### 5.2 Dynamical Migration Mechanism
Global zeros $\zeta(s)=0$ satisfy the equilibrium equation:
$$R_1(s; N) = - [R_2(s; N) + R_3(s; N)]$$

**Conclusion:** As the system undergoes grammatical expansion (increasing $N$), the high-frequency random phases generated by the residue $R_3$ perform non-linear interference with $R_1$ in the complex plane. Constrained by the symmetry of the functional equation, this interference forms a stable attractor at $\sigma = \frac{1}{2}$, forcing zeros to leave the imaginary axis and migrate toward the critical line.
